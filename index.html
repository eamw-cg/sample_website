My Learn Enough™ Story

Learn Enough™ to Be Dangerous is a leader in the movement to teach technical sophistication, the seemingly magical ability to take command of your computer and get it to do your bidding. This includes everything from command lines and coding, to guessing keyboard shortcuts, Googling error messages, and knowing when to just reboot the darn thing.

The team at Learn Enough™ believes that there are at least a billion people who can benefit from learning technical sophistication, and probably more.

I am one of those billion people, and I have absolutely benefited from completing several Learn Enough™ tutorials already, with more to come!

Learn Enough™ Background

Learn Enough to be Dangerous is an outgrowth of Ruby on Rails Tutorial and the Softcover publishing platform.

This page is being created as an exercise for the Learn Enough™ HTML to Be Dangerous tutorial, which teaches the basics of Hyper Text Markup Language, the universal language of the World Wide Web.

This site is a near duplicate of the sample site created to support that tutorial, which I have only changed to include information about my experience and goals for taking these tutorials in the first place.

Learn Enough™ Founders

Learn Enough to Be Dangerous was founded in 2015 by Michael Hartl, Lee Donahoe, and Nick Merwin. They believe that the kind of technical sophistication taught by the Learn Enough™ tutorials can benefit at least a billion people, and probably more.
