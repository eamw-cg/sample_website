My Learn Enough™ Story

Learn Enough™ to Be Dangerous is a leader in the movement to teach technical sophistication, the seemingly magical ability to take command of your computer and get it to do your bidding. This includes everything from command lines and coding, to guessing keyboard shortcuts, Googling error messages, and knowing when to just reboot the darn thing.

The team at Learn Enough™ believes that there are at least a billion people who can benefit from learning technical sophistication, and probably more.

I am one of those billion people, and I have absolutely benefited from completing several Learn Enough™ tutorials already, with more to come!

To join their movement, sign up for the Learn Enough™ official email list now.

Learn Enough™ Background

Learn Enough to be Dangerous is an outgrowth of the Ruby on Rails Tutorial and the Softcover publishing platform.

This page is being created as an exercise for the Learn Enough HTML to Be Dangerous tutorial, which teaches the basics of Hyper Text Markup Language, the universal language of the World Wide Web. Other related tutorials can be found at learnenough.com

This site is a near duplicate of the sample site created to support that tutorial, which I have only changed to include information about my experience in taking these tutorials, as well as my goals for learning these skills.

Learn Enough™ Founders

Learn Enough to Be Dangerous was founded in 2015 by Michael Hartl, Lee Donahoe, and Nick Merwin. They believe that the kind of technical sophistication taught by the Learn Enough™ tutorials can benefit at least a billion people, and probably more.

Michael Hartl

Michael Hartl is the creator of the Ruby on Rails Tutorial and the principal author of the Learn Enough to Be Dangerous introductory sequence. He is an advanced student of Krav Maga and has a three-step plan for world comination. Rumors that he's secretly a supervillian are slightly exaggerated.
